The TSITP Verdict 🌊

If you want peak American Girl 4th: Southampton — parade, Grucci fireworks, Hamptons farm stands, a big group house. Classic, cinematic, the works.

If you want the most fun social weekend: Fire Island — no cars, ferry vibes, everyone on the same strip of beach, bars open late.

If you want TSITP summer-core: Montauk — bikes, surfboards, Umbrella Beach fireworks, Ruschmeyers pig roast, end-of-the-world energy. Belly was definitely here.

If you want the most budget-friendly + farm day: Westport RI — lowest price, Berry Hill Farm blueberry picking, quiet New England beach. Drive is doable with a rental.

If you want New England 4th vibes with room to breathe: Narragansett — fireworks over the ocean, big house, RI farm day possible, Newport nearby.

Hamptons, Long Island, NY

Southampton

~$500 / person
Sleeps 6 + hammock/couch
TLDR: The most classically American 4th you can do. Southampton runs one of Long Island's biggest 4th of July parades down North Main Street, and the Grucci fireworks over Shinnecock Bay are legendary. Think Hamptons farm stands, sun-bleached linen, beach chairs, and the parade route filling with locals. This is the aspirational summer postcard.
Getting There from NYC
TrainLIRR from Penn Station / Atlantic Terminal → Southampton (direct on Montauk Branch). ~2–2.5 hrs. $20–30 one way.
At DestinationNo car needed in the village — Uber/Lyft or bikes. Rent bikes to explore town and beaches.
Pro TipBook LIRR early — holiday weekend trains fill up fast. Leave NYC by 7am on July 3.
Itinerary — 2 Days (Fri July 3 – Sun July 5)

Ocean Bay Park / Ocean Beach, Fire Island, NY

Fire Island

~$480–630 / person
Sleeps 8–10 (two listings)
TLDR: No cars, a ferry, and a barrier island packed with bungalows, beach bars, and community spirit. The Point O'Woods fireworks light up the Great South Bay from a barge — visible from almost every community on the island. Ocean Beach has a children's parade, bars along Bay Walk, and the kind of energy where you feel like you've stepped into another world. The most fun, social option on this list.
Getting There from NYC
TrainLIRR from Penn Station → Bay Shore (Babylon Branch). ~1 hr. Then a 25-min ferry to Ocean Beach or Ocean Bay Park. ~$30 train + ~$12–15 ferry each way.
No CarsEverything on the island is walkable or by wagon. Bring a wagon/cart for luggage — a staple of Fire Island life.
TipBook round-trip ferry tickets in advance for the holiday weekend. Last ferry back is late — confirm times.
Itinerary — 2 Days (Fri July 3 – Sun July 5)

5pm🍺Sunset on the bay side — cocktails at Houser's (Ocean Bay Park) or Matt's (Ocean Beach).
7pm🔥BBQ at the house — pick up supplies from the small island grocery.
10pm🎶Late-night bar crawl on Bay Walk. Fire Island nightlife is legendary on the 4th.

7pm🌅Cocktail hour on the bay — lay down blankets for the best fireworks view of the trip.
9pm🎆Point O'Woods fireworks launched from a barge on the Great South Bay. Visible from shorelines across the island. Spectacular.

East End, Long Island, NY

Montauk ⭐ Most TSITP

~$555 / person
Sleeps 8, 3 min to beach
TLDR: The end of Long Island — a surfy, sun-weathered fishing town that goes hard on the 4th. The "Stars Over Montauk" Grucci fireworks at Umbrella Beach (Kirk Park) are one of the East End's most beloved shows, launching over the Atlantic at 9pm. Ruschmeyers runs a legendary pig roast with lawn games and a DJ on the 4th. Rent bikes, hit the surf, explore the harbor. This is the closest thing to Cousins Beach you'll find in the northeast.
Getting There from NYC
TrainLIRR from Penn Station → Montauk (Montauk Branch, direct). ~3–3.5 hrs. $30–40 one way. Book early — holiday trains sell out.
At DestinationRent bikes when you arrive — Montauk Bike Shop or Puff 'N Putt. Everything is bikeable. Uber/Lyft for evenings.
BBQ + GearHouse includes BBQ and beach gear. Bring beach games.
Itinerary — 3 Days (Thu July 2 – Sun July 5) ✦ Recommended

Rhode Island

Narragansett, RI

~$512 / person
Sleeps 12, 5 min to beach
TLDR: A classic New England beach town — think weathered shingles, lobster shacks, and Atlantic waves. The fireworks launch over Narragansett Town Beach on July 3rd at dusk (perfect timing for your trip), and Newport — 30 min away — fires off over Newport Harbor on the 4th at 9:15pm. You're also a short drive from Rhode Island's incredible PYO berry farms, and the house sleeps 12, so everyone has real beds.
Getting There from NYC
Rental CarBest option — rent a couple of cars in NYC (~$100–150/day split). ~3 hr drive on I-95. Highly recommended for RI options.
BusPeter Pan or FlixBus to Providence, then Uber/Lyft to Narragansett. ~3.5–4 hrs total.
At DestinationCar useful for farm day and Newport trip, but walkable to town beach.

Rhode Island / SE Massachusetts border

Westport, RI

~$347 / person
Sleeps 6 + couch space
TLDR: The quiet, local, genuinely agricultural option. Westport (technically on the MA/RI border, near Fall River) sits close to Horseneck State Beach and is surrounded by working farms — including Berry Hill Farm, which opens for blueberry picking in late June through August. Fireworks are a short drive to Narragansett or Newport. It's the most intimate, lowest-key option and the most budget-friendly by a significant margin.
Getting There from NYC
Rental CarBest option — ~3 hrs from NYC on I-95 N. Rent 2 cars. Worth it for the farm access and flexibility.
BusFlixBus/Peter Pan to Providence or Fall River, then Uber. More hassle, but doable.
At DestinationNeed a car to get around — Westport is rural/coastal. Farm is a short drive. Horseneck Beach is close.
Itinerary — 2 Days (Fri July 3 – Sun July 5)

Suffolk County, Long Island, NY

Rocky Point, NY

~$512 / person
Sleeps 8
TLDR: Rocky Point is on Long Island Sound (North Shore), not the ocean — so it's calmer water, and the vibe is more local LI community than Hamptons glam. The big draw for your group: proximity to LI's amazing farm country (it's surrounded by farms and farm stands), and Manny's Beach Club runs a 4th of July fireworks celebration. Less Hamptons, more honest Long Island summer.
Getting There from NYC
TrainLIRR from Penn Station → Ronkonkoma or Port Jefferson (Port Jefferson Branch). ~1.5–2 hrs. Then Uber/Lyft to Rocky Point (~15–20 min, ~$25). No direct stop.
Rental CarRecommended if you want flexibility for farms and beaches. Rocky Point is spread out.
TipRocky Point is more car-oriented than the other LI options. Budget for Ubers or bring a car.
